A Voice of Unparalleled Grace

Krishnan Nair Shantakumari Chithra, known professionally as K.S. Chithra or Chitra, is an Indian playback singer and Carnatic musician. Often referred to as the "Nightingale of South India," she is celebrated for her melodious voice, exceptional vocal range, and ability to sing with remarkable clarity and emotion across multiple languages.

Born on July 27, 1963, in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, Chithra began her musical journey at a very young age under the guidance of her father, the late Krishnan Nair. Her formal training in Carnatic music provided the foundation for what would become one of the most illustrious careers in Indian playback singing.

With a career spanning over four decades, Chithra has recorded more than 25,000 songs in various Indian and foreign languages. Her versatility in singing different genres - from classical compositions to peppy film numbers - has made her a favorite among music directors across India.
