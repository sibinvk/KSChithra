M.G. Radhakrishnan

1979 - 2000s

300+
Songs
Malayalam
Primary Language

The music director who discovered Chithra and introduced her to Malayalam playback singing in 1979. He recognized her potential and gave her the platform to showcase her extraordinary talent.

Notable Works:

  • "Angopangam" (Devasuram) - Semi-classical masterpiece recorded in single take
  • Debut in "Kummatti" (1979)
  • Early films: Attahasam, Snehapoorvam Meera, Njan Ekananu
  • Numerous Malayalam film albums throughout the 1980s-90s

Ilaiyaraaja

1985 - Present

1000+
Songs
Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada
Languages

One of the most prolific collaborations in Indian cinema history. Ilaiyaraaja recognized Chithra's talent in the mid-1980s and made her a regular voice in Tamil cinema. Together they created thousands of timeless melodies.

Iconic Songs:

  • "Padariye Padippariye" (Sindhu Bhairavi, 1985) - First National Award
  • First Telugu song: "Paadalenu Pallavaina" (Sindhu Bhairavi dubbed version)
  • "Ethetho Ennam Valarthen" - First collaboration (1986)
  • "Yaaro Sonnaangalaam" - Popular Tamil melody
  • Hundreds of Tamil and Telugu film songs

A.R. Rahman

1992 - Present

200+
Songs
Tamil, Hindi, Malayalam, Telugu
Languages

The collaboration that brought Chithra nationwide and international stardom. Rahman could record up to 20 songs in a day with her during her prime, testament to her versatility and professionalism.

Blockbuster Hits:

  • "Chinna Chinna Aasai" (Roja, 1992) - Made her a nationwide star
  • "Kannalane" (Bombay, 1995) - Hindi version made her identifiable across India
  • "Ooh La La La" (Minsara Kanavu, 1997) - Second National Award with Rahman
  • "Malargale" (Love Birds) - Challenging composition
  • "Yenge Enadhu Kavithai" (Kandukondein Kandukondein)
  • "Kannamoochi Yennada" - Complex raga changes
  • "Malargal Ketten" (OK Kanmani, 2015) - Rahman waited for her return to record this

Raveendran

1980s - 2005

250+
Songs
Malayalam
Primary Language

A major force in Malayalam cinema, Raveendran's melodious compositions perfectly complemented Chithra's voice, creating numerous evergreen hits.

Memorable Songs:

  • "Aalolam Chaanchaadum" (Aduthaduthu, 1984)
  • "Pularkala Sundara" (Oru May Masapulariyil) - Wistful melody
  • "Chandanam Manakkunna" (Achuvettante Veedu) - Exquisite composition
  • "Vaishaka Sandhye" (Nadodikattu) - Comforting classic
  • "Shyama Meghame" (Adhipan) - Perfect rendition
  • "Poo Veeno Pooveeno" (Deshadanakili Karayaarilla, 1986)
  • "Gopika Vasantham" (His Highness Abdullah, 1990) - Based on Shanmukhapriya raga

Johnson

1986 - 2011

180+
Songs
Malayalam
Language

Johnson was so impressed with Chithra's version of "Madhuram Jeevamritha Bindu" (originally composed for Yesudas) that he shook her hands in admiration. Dominated Malayalam cinema in the 1990s with soulful melodies.

Hit Songs:

  • "Pala Poove" (Njan Gandharvan, 1991) - Based on Madhuradhwani raga
  • "Kannaadikkayyil" (Pavam Pavam Rajakumaran, 1990)
  • "Madhuram Jeevamritha Bindu" (Chenkol) - Overwhelmed Johnson
  • "Ponveene Ennullil" (Thalavattam, 1986) - Duet & solo versions
  • Numerous Malayalam romantic and devotional songs
